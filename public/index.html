De La Calexo Africa

Pioneered by an emerging environmentalist Caleb Burudi, De La Calexo Africa has been committed to planting trees to save the earth from climate change. The story of De La Calexo Africa is one of patriotism and nationalism. Born in the fringes of Kakamega County, Shikulu village, Caleb developed interest for planting trees at a tender age. In 2018, he begun the journey of establishing tree nurseries, nurturing seedlings and donating them to nearby communities. These efforts marked the beginning of a humanitarian call that has since snowballed across the country growing trees and creating awareness on the need to preserve and restock our forests cover. Inspired by a directive made by the former president Uhuru Kenyatta, which sought to achieve 10% forest cover by 2020, De La Calexo Africa has unveiled a number of initiatives to reinforce efforts by government authorities to bring this promise to fruition. Some of our dreams to deliver;

To support the implementation of Presidential directive of planting 5 Billion trees in 5 years.

To create employment and opportunities for the youth, women and people with disability.

To promote the planting of trees as a lifestyle.

To raise awareness on the dangers of deforestation.

To monitor and support communal afforestation efforts through innovative technologies.

To create resilience and adaptation to the climate change.

To promote diverse fuel sustainability and innovation.

To mobilize resources for the expansion and restocking of forests.

About De La Calexo Africa

Our Mission

De La Calexo aims to produce millions of tree seedlings for afforestation in Kenya for mitigating climate change.

What are our Objectives at De La Calexo?

1. To support the implementation of Presidential directive of planting 5 Billion trees in 5 years.
2. To create employment and opportunities for the youth, women and people with disability.
3. To raise awareness on the dangers of deforestation.
4. To monitor and support communal afforestation efforts through innovative technologies.
5. To create resilience and adaptation to the climate change.
6. To promote diverse fuel sustainability and innovation.
7. To mobilize resources for the expansion and restocking of forests.

What are some of the milestones?

Growing of trees in all Police Stations in Nairobi County, Kakamega County, Vihiga County, Kajiado County, part of Bungoma County, Nakuru County and more.

Our Vision

Our vision is to promote continuous planting of trees and restock forests across vulnerable communities to combat the adverse effects of climate change and be a leading player in tree seedlings production in East Africa, Central Africa and beyond.

Who are our strategic partners?

Some of our strategic partners include Kenya Forest Service (KFS) Kakamega and Kajiado Counties, National Youth Service (NYS), Kenya Forest Research Institute (KEFRI) Muguga, Ministry of Interior, Ministry of Environment, Forestry and Climate Change, Ministry of Gender, Ministry of Youths, Rotary Club, Private Partners and Non-State Actors.

What tree varieties do we plant?

Some of the trees we plant are Acacia (Naivasha Yellow), Croton Megalocarpus (Mukinduri), Makhamia Lutea, Eucalyptus (Blue Gum), Cypress, Griveria (Mukima) and Bamboo.
